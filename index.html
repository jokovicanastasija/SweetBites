First cakes

The ancient Egyptians were good bakers and from bread with the addition of honey they made a change in the previous production of bread. In fact, there was no difference between cakes and bread for a very long time, although the sweet forms were made from different ingredients such as fruit, seeds and wine and were served as a dessert.

Cakes as we know them today have a soft and stable structure. The Romans probably perfected the practice of adding yeast to dough, and later, in the 16th century, the Italians added eggs and butter to the whole story. Both methods created the dough of the cake itself, but the process was time consuming and quite complicated. By the mid-1800s, baking soda, a baking powder, was included in the production, which facilitated the process of making cakes.

Later, the confectioners started adding spices and fat to the dough, and slowly, the cake got its present appearance. In addition, cakes and pies began to be served as a mandatory dessert, after the main course

In the middle of the 19th century, the wave of founding confectioneries in European cities began, and then some of the most popular cakes were created.
